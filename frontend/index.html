Before you start — set up AI scoring

ALDA uses AI to do two things:

  • Understand your research question — extracting keywords, date ranges, and criteria automatically
  • Score each result for relevance — so the most useful sources appear first

This requires a free API key from an AI provider. Setup takes about 2 minutes.

Without AI, ALDA still searches and returns results — but won’t score them for relevance and will use basic keyword extraction for your brief.

Drag & drop your file here, or click to select

Accepted formats: CSV spreadsheet, JSON, or DuckDB database file

How to prepare a CSV file

Each row is one source. You need at least one of: a URL or a DOI (a digital object identifier — looks like 10.1234/example).

Optional columns you can include:

  • title — the title of the source
  • authors — semicolon-separated, e.g. Smith J; Jones A; Lee K
  • year — publication year, e.g. 2022
  • abstract — the abstract or summary text
  • venue — journal name, conference, or publisher

Example (first two rows of your CSV):

url,title,authors,year
https://example.org/paper1,My Paper Title,Smith J; Jones A,2022

Export Your Results

Download your sources as a spreadsheet you can open in Excel or Google Sheets, or as a structured JSON file for developers. The PRISMA section generates a flow table required by many academic journals.

PRISMA Flow Statistics

PRISMA (Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses) is the international standard for documenting your search process. Most academic journals require a PRISMA flow diagram when you submit a systematic review.

Thematic Clusters

ALDA groups your sources into themes automatically — useful for spotting the main topics in your results. Larger tags mean more sources belong to that theme. Click any tag to go to your results filtered by that theme.
